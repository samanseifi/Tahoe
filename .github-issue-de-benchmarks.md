**Title:** Add smoke-test benchmarks for all 7 uncovered dielectric elastomer element variants

**Labels:** testing

---

## Context

Tahoe ships 9 distinct dielectric elastomer element/material families in `development/src/elements/`.
A benchmark XML is the only way to catch regressions like the `extern "C"` symbol-collision NaN
fixed in commit `61fa1d5f` before they reach users.

7 variants are completely uncovered.

## Known issues to fix alongside benchmarks

- **`dielectric_elastomer` (3D, #1)**: single-hex passes; multi-element mesh aborts with
  heap corruption (`double free` with SPOOLES, `malloc: invalid size` with MUMPS).
  The asymmetric 4-DOF/node tangent assembly is suspect.
- **`dielectric_elastomer_Q1P0` (3D, #3)**: NaN from `extern "C"` symbol collision fixed
  in commit `61fa1d5f`. The `Young_Modulus`/`Poisson` XML parameters that the material
  registers but never uses in stress/tangent should be removed.

## Proposed work

For each uncovered variant, add at minimum a **single-element smoke test** at
`benchmark_XML/level.4/single_element/` and wire it into CTest:

- Apply a uniform electric field + mechanical stretch.
- Verify convergence in ≤ 3 Newton iterations (no NaN, no crash).
- For visco variants (#5, #6, #7): also verify that the internal viscous history
  variables (`fC_v`) update and that the dissipated energy is non-zero.
- For surface/elastocapillary variants (#8, #9): verify the surface-energy
  contribution is non-trivial (different from the same mesh with surface effects off).

## Priority

Medium. The 3D Q1P0 family is the most active; visco and surface variants are
research features that predate the CMake build and have never had CI coverage.
